Consequence ladder — non-performance

First miss → Verbal + logged note
One missed deliverable or 80–89% policy test. Verbal conversation same week. Note logged in performance file. No ambiguity about expectations.
Second miss (same, 30 days) → Written warning
Pattern established. Written warning issued. CSM submits self-corrective plan within 5 days. KAM notified. Director reviews in next 1:1.
Third miss or Q-score < 60 → PIP initiated
30–60 day formal Performance Improvement Plan. Specific, measurable, time-bound targets. Weekly check-ins. Mentorship support assigned immediately.
PIP failure or 2 amber quarters → Exit
Role reassignment or separation. This is structural, not punitive. Act decisively — a non-performer costs the team, clients, and every KAM they support.

Reward ladder — high performance

2 green months in a row → Recognition
Public acknowledgement in team meeting. Peer recognition nomination. Added to stretch project list.
Elite band (90+) → Advancement trigger
Compensation review initiated. Senior CSM shadow program offered. First consideration for team lead openings.
2 elite quarters → Fast-track
Formal promotion discussion with HR. Expanded account portfolio. Invited to director-level strategy sessions.
Tough conversation protocol
Direct, same week. No softening preamble. Lead with the specific missed behaviour. State the expected standard. State the consequence if unmet. Confirm understanding. Document immediately. This is not optional and cannot be delegated.
